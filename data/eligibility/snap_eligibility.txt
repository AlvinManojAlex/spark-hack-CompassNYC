SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (SNAP) - NEW YORK STATE

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) issues electronic benefits that can be used like cash to purchase food. SNAP helps low-income working people, senior citizens, the disabled and others feed their families. Eligibility and benefit levels are based on household size, income and other factors.

SNAP benefits are provided through an Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card, similar to a bank debit card or credit card. If you are eligible, an EBT account is set up for you, and every month your benefits will be deposited right into your account.

INCOME ELIGIBILITY GUIDELINES

IMPORTANT: If your household's gross income is below these amounts, it does not ensure that you will be eligible for a benefit. A SNAP budget must be calculated for your household in order to determine program eligibility and your actual benefit amount. The only way to determine if your household is eligible for SNAP benefits is to apply.

Gross income means a household's total, non-excluded income, before any deductions have been made such as taxes or other withholdings. Net income means gross income minus allowable deductions.

All income guidelines are based upon SNAP Standards effective October 1, 2025.

ASSET/SAVINGS REQUIREMENTS

Most households applying for SNAP no longer have to pass a savings/resource test in order to get SNAP benefits. This means that the household's assets (stocks, savings and retirement accounts, etc.) are not considered when determining eligibility.


COLLEGE STUDENT ELIGIBILITY

To participate in SNAP, a student in college at least half time must meet the income eligibility for SNAP and ONE of the following criteria:

- Under age 19 or age 50 or older
- Have a physical or mental disability
- Work at least 20 hours a week in paid employment
- Participate in a State or Federally financed work study program during the regular school year
- Participate in an on-the-job training program
- Care for a child under the age of 6, OR care for a child between ages 6 to 11 and lack the necessary childcare to allow them to attend school and work 20 hours per week or participate in work study
- Single parent enrolled full time in college and taking care of a child under age 12
- Receive Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)
- Attend a SUNY or CUNY comprehensive college, technology college, or community college and are enrolled in a qualified career and technical education program
- Attend any of the 10 Educational Opportunity Centers (EOC) in New York State and are enrolled in a career and technical education program, remedial course, basic adult education, literacy, or English as a second language


WORK REQUIREMENTS

ABAWD (Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents) Work Requirements: The ABAWD rules for SNAP participants are in effect for all counties beginning March 1, 2026. ABAWDs must start meeting the ABAWD work rules in March to keep their SNAP benefits.


RECERTIFICATION PROCESS

If you already get SNAP benefits in New York State, you will receive a recertification packet in the mail approximately two months prior to the end of your certification period so that you can recertify (renew your benefits). This recertification packet will include a SNAP application and a scheduled interview appointment on page one.

Three important steps in the recertification process:

1. You must submit a Recertification application before you can be interviewed on your scheduled interview appointment shown on page one of your recertification packet. You can submit your SNAP Recertification application by mail, from your home by using the internet (myBenefits.ny.gov), by fax, or in person at your local department of social services.

2. If you submit your application AFTER the scheduled interview appointment on page one of your recertification packet, then it is YOUR responsibility to reschedule your interview appointment before the end of your recertification period.

3. After you submit your application, you must be interviewed. After your interview, you may be asked to submit additional documents related to your income or circumstances.

EBT CARD SECURITY

Freeze your card to protect your benefits from being stolen. Use the freeze/unfreeze card feature to help secure your EBT card and protect your benefits from scam-related theft.


WHAT CAN BE PURCHASED WITH SNAP

SNAP monthly benefits can be used to purchase food at authorized retail food stores. SNAP benefits can be used to order nutritious food online at participating stores in New York State that participate in USDA's Online Purchasing Pilot.
